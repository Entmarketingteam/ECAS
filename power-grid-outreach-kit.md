## TARGET NICHES — TIER 1 (LEAD WITH THESE)

### Niche A: Substation EPC Contractors
- **Why first:** Highest deal size/LTV ratio. $5M–$50M projects. A single new utility client = $50M+ over a decade.
- **ICP:** CEO/VP BD at 50–500 employee EPC firm doing $20M–$150M revenue. Mostly regional. 1–3 major utility clients they rely on.
- **Pain:** Revenue concentration (1–2 utility clients). Not on short-list for new utilities. Can't explain what makes them different.
- **Hook to find:** Recent project completion press release or LinkedIn post. SAM.gov contract award. ENR Top Specialty Contractor ranking.
- **Lead magnet:** "Contract Signal Report — 5 active substation/transmission projects in your region sourced from FERC, USASpending.gov, and state utility commission filings."

### Niche B: Solar EPC Contractors (Utility-Scale)
- **Why second:** High composite score. IRA/DOE tailwinds. Growing fast and spending on biz dev. More open to marketing than traditional grid EPCs.
- **ICP:** CEO/COO at utility-scale solar developer-contractor doing $10M–$100M revenue. Looking to win more utility PPAs and data center power contracts.
- **Pain:** Interconnection queue backlog. Competing on price. Not differentiated vs. 50 other solar EPCs.
- **Hook to find:** PJM/MISO/CAISO interconnection queue applications. Solar industry press (Canary Media, PV Magazine). LinkedIn posts about project milestones.
- **Lead magnet:** "Grid Upgrade Pipeline Report — upcoming IRA/DOE-funded renewable interconnection projects in your region, with timeline, project owner, and procurement method."

### Niche C: EV Charging Station Installers (Commercial/Fleet)
- **Why third:** Shorter sales cycle to close (45 days vs. 90–120 for substations). More reachable on digital. Faster feedback loop.
- **ICP:** Owner/VP Operations at commercial EV charging installer doing $5M–$50M. Targeting fleet operators, municipalities, commercial real estate.
- **Pain:** NEVI formula program confusion. Winning state/federal grants vs. private contracts. Competing against EVSE manufacturer-installers.
- **Hook to find:** LinkedIn posts about completed charging stations. NEVI grant award announcements. SAM.gov EV infrastructure awards.
- **Lead magnet:** "NEVI & Federal EV Funding Pipeline — active DOT/DOE EV infrastructure funding opportunities in your state with application timelines and award histories."

## DREAM 100 PROSPECT SCORING RUBRIC

Score each prospect 1–10 before investing Phase 1 research time:

| Signal | Points |
|--------|--------|
| $20M+ revenue (ENR ranking, job postings) | 3 |
| Hiring Project Developer / BD / Preconstruction | 2 |
| Recently completed a notable project (press release/LinkedIn) | 2 |
| Active on LinkedIn (CEO posts about industry topics) | 1 |
| Website hasn't been updated in 3+ years | 1 |
| References only 1–2 utility clients | 1 |

**8–10:** Run full Phase 1 research. High priority.
**5–7:** Nurture with signal report lead magnet, lower personalization.
**<5:** Add to bulk list, monitor for scoring trigger events.

## VALUE-ADDED ASSETS (Deliverables to Send After They Say Yes)

### Asset 1: Contract Signal Report (Phase 3 Deliverable)
What to include:
- 5 active grid/energy projects in their territory
- Source (FERC filing #, USASpending.gov award, state commission docket #)
- Project owner (utility, municipality, or developer)
- Estimated value and scope
- Decision-maker contact (if available from SAM.gov or LinkedIn)
- Procurement stage (pre-RFP, active RFP, award phase)

### Asset 2: Competitor Digital Presence Audit
What to include:
- Side-by-side comparison: their website vs. top 3 competitors
- LinkedIn presence: company followers, CEO activity, case study posts
- Google result for "[their specialty] [their region] contractor"
- Specific gaps that represent an immediate opportunity

### Asset 3: Grid Project Pipeline Report (Regional)
What to include:
- 10–15 upcoming projects in their state/region
- Funding source (IRA, DOE, state utility commission, private)
- Estimated timeline to procurement
- Project owner and contact info
- Notes on which competitors are already pursuing this project

## KEY METRICS TO TRACK

Don't measure leads. Measure:
- Pre-qualification applications submitted to new utilities
- New utility relationships initiated (introductory calls held)
- RFP invitations received from utilities they weren't previously on the short-list for
- Digital share of voice vs. top 3 competitors in region

Present these in discovery calls. It's the language power grid contractors understand.
