## 4. DISCOVERY CALL FRAMEWORK

**Format:** 30 minutes. This is a diagnostic conversation, not a pitch. You're trying to determine fit for the guarantee — not close the deal.

---

### Opening — 2 Minutes

*"Thanks for the time. Here's what I'd like to do in the next 30 minutes — I'll spend the first 15 understanding your current BD process and pipeline, and then I'll walk you through specifically how ECAS works and whether it's a fit for where you're trying to go. I'll be direct with you about whether I think we can deliver for your situation. Sound good?"*

Establish credibility briefly: *"We work specifically with mid-tier power and grid infrastructure EPCs in the $20M–$300M range. Our focus is Dominion territory and ERCOT right now. That context will help frame what I'm going to ask you."*

---

### Discovery Questions — 10–12 Questions

Work through these naturally. Not a survey — a conversation. Listen more than you talk.

1. "Walk me through how you currently find and qualify new contract opportunities. What's the actual process?"

2. "How much of your current pipeline came from relationships you already had versus RFPs you responded to cold?"

3. "What percentage of the RFPs you respond to do you win? And do you know what your win rate looks like on opportunities where you had a pre-existing relationship with the GC or owner?"

4. "Who are your ideal clients — utilities, data center developers, independent power producers, other? And within that, what's the contract size range where you're most competitive?"

5. "How far in advance do you typically get visibility on projects before they go to formal bid?"

6. "Do you have anyone on the team actively monitoring FERC filings, interconnection queues, or utility capex announcements? Or is your signal flow mostly conference relationships and referrals?"

7. "What does your backlog look like right now — and where do you want it to be in 18 months?"

8. "Have you pursued any Dominion or AEP/ERCOT work in the last two years? What was the experience?"

9. "What's the bottleneck for you right now — finding the right opportunities, or winning the ones you're already in front of?"

10. "If I showed you a credible project signal 90 days before a formal RFP, what would you actually do with it? Do you have the BD and preconstruction bandwidth to act on pre-solicitation intelligence?"

11. "What would a successful 12 months look like from a business development standpoint — specific number, contract types, geographies?"

12. "What's made you skeptical of or open to outside BD support in the past?"

---

### Diagnosis — How to Identify Fit

**Green flags — proceed to pitch:**
- Win rate on cold bids is below 20% but they want to grow
- Current BD process is reactive — conferences, referrals, public solicitations
- No systematic FERC/interconnection monitoring
- Backlog gap: they want $20M–$50M more in the next 18 months than their current pipeline supports
- Has preconstruction/estimating capacity that can absorb new projects
- Target geography overlaps Dominion or ERCOT
- Open to outcome-based fee structure

**Yellow flags — dig deeper before proceeding:**
- Pipeline is "full" — ask: full through when? What's the 18-month view?
- Has a BD team — ask: what signals are they currently working from?
- Has utility relationships — ask: are those relationships generating pre-RFP intelligence or just project knowledge after RFP release?

**Red flags — exit gracefully:**
- Revenue under $15M (guarantee math doesn't work)
- No preconstruction or estimating capacity to absorb new work
- No appetite for a performance-based fee structure
- Primary work is residential, light commercial, or non-grid
- Geographically limited to markets outside Dominion/ERCOT with no expansion intent

---

# ECAS — Enterprise Contract Acquisition System
### Pre-RFP Signal Intelligence for Mid-Tier Power & Grid Infrastructure Contractors

---

**What ECAS Is**

ECAS is a signal-driven business development system for mid-tier electrical EPCs, substation contractors, and transmission builders. We monitor public regulatory filings and utility capital programs to identify active projects 60–90 days before they reach formal RFP — and then position qualified contractors in the pre-solicitation conversations that determine who wins.

---

**How the Signal Engine Works**

We pull from four primary signal sources daily: FERC dockets, interconnection queue filings, utility rate case submissions, and land permit databases. We layer those against utility earnings call transcripts and PPA announcements to build a project probability map — which signals represent near-term contract opportunity versus long-term planning noise. When a signal cluster indicates a project is entering active pre-development, we identify the relevant utility contacts and begin the positioning work for our client contractors.

A live example: The Dominion 500kV reinforcement filing in Loudoun County (FERC, February 2026) represents approximately $400M–$700M in substation and transmission construction work. It will go to formal RFP in Q4 2026 at the earliest. Pre-solicitation contractor conversations are happening now.

---

**What "Positioned Before RFP" Actually Means**

Here is what it looks like in practice. Dominion Energy files for a new 500kV line segment in Loudoun County in February 2026, driven by five new data center interconnection requests totaling 1.8 GW. We identify that filing within 48 hours. We map it to the Dominion project development team managing the Northern Virginia load growth program. We identify that team's procurement lead. Over the next 60 days, we introduce our client contractor through a warm channel — a technical conversation, a site visit invitation, a targeted RFI response — so that by the time the formal solicitation documents are published, our client is not an unknown respondent but a known entity with a documented track record of engagement. On average, that distinction moves a contractor from a 12–18% win rate on cold bids to a 35–45% win rate on solicitations where they have pre-existing relationships. That is the value of pre-RFP positioning.

---

**The Guarantee**

We guarantee two signed enterprise contracts at $500,000 or above within 180 days of engagement. If we do not deliver two qualifying signed contracts within the engagement period, we extend our services at no additional retainer cost until the benchmark is met. Performance fees apply only to signed contracts — not proposals, introductions, or letters of intent.

---

**SLA Requirements — What We Need From You**

This is a joint operating model. We need three commitments from your side:

1. A designated point of contact (BD lead, preconstruction director, or project executive) who responds to qualified introductions within 48 hours.
2. Active participation in pre-RFP positioning conversations — site visits, technical RFI responses, relationship-building meetings with utility contacts as arranged.
3. Honest, real-time feedback from your estimating team on project fit and bid capacity so we are not pursuing opportunities your team cannot execute.

We cannot deliver the guarantee without these commitments. We are transparent about that before engagement starts.

---

**Pricing**

Monthly retainer: $12,000–$15,000 depending on geographic scope and signal monitoring complexity. Performance fee: $25,000–$50,000 per signed qualifying contract (fee range based on contract value). The retainer covers signal monitoring, pre-RFP intelligence, outreach execution, and relationship facilitation. The performance fee is paid only upon contract execution.

---

**The First 30 Days**

Days 1–7: Signal map build. We pull the current FERC and interconnection queue data for your target geography, identify active pre-RFP projects in your contract range, and present a prioritized opportunity map.

Days 8–21: Introduction phase. We initiate contact with the relevant utility or developer contacts on the highest-priority signals. We prepare your team for the first pre-RFP conversations with background on project context, key decision-makers, and recommended positioning.

## 6. OBJECTION HANDLING MASTER GUIDE

Each entry: the objection, the underlying concern, the reframe, and the response.

---

### "Our pipeline is already full."

**Underlying concern:** Capacity constraint is real or they don't see a problem worth solving.

**Reframe:** Pipeline fullness is a snapshot, not a guarantee. The question is what the 18-month view looks like and whether the contract mix is what they want.

**Response:** *"That's a good position to be in. My question is — full through when? Most mid-tier EPCs we work with have 6–9 months of backlog visibility but significant uncertainty after that. The pre-RFP cycle in Dominion and ERCOT territory is 12–18 months ahead of contract execution. If you're not positioned in those conversations now, the work available to you in Q2 2027 is being allocated to other contractors today. I'm not saying you have a problem right now. I'm saying the window to shape what your pipeline looks like in 18 months is open right now, and it won't be in six months."*

---

### "We already have relationships with the utilities."

**Underlying concern:** They believe their existing relationships are sufficient and external positioning adds no value.

**Reframe:** Relationships are necessary but not sufficient. The question is whether those relationships are generating pre-solicitation intelligence — specific project signals — or general goodwill.

**Response:** *"That's an asset, not a reason to stop there. The contractors who consistently win utility work have relationships AND systematic signal monitoring. The relationship gets you in the room. The signal tells you which room to be in before anyone else shows up. When Dominion's Q4 earnings call cited $4.3B in grid capex, did that translate into specific project intelligence for your BD team within two weeks? If yes, you may not need us. If no — if that kind of upstream signal isn't flowing into your preconstruction pipeline — then there's a gap we can close. The relationships you have are actually the reason we could deliver faster than average."*

---

### "We're too small for something like this."

**Underlying concern:** Either genuine resource constraint or a belief that pre-RFP BD is only for the big contractors.

**Reframe:** The guarantee math works best for firms in the $20M–$150M range — large enough to execute $500K+ contracts, small enough that two new enterprise contracts represent meaningful revenue growth.

**Response:** *"The firms this system is designed for are exactly in your size range. A $500K contract is meaningful for a $30M EPC in a way that it isn't for a $500M firm. The pre-RFP intelligence we're building is specifically calibrated to mid-tier contract opportunities — the $500K–$5M range that the large EPCs don't want to pursue and the small subs can't execute. If you're doing $20M–$100M in revenue and you want to grow 20–30% in the next 24 months, two new enterprise contracts in 180 days is exactly the mechanism. The system is sized for you, not for Quanta."*

---

### "How is this different from hiring a BD person?"

**Underlying concern:** They're comparing ECAS to an internal hire and doing the cost math. A BD person at $120K–$180K all-in is roughly comparable to the retainer.

**Reframe:** A BD person brings relationships and execution capacity. They don't bring a signal monitoring infrastructure, a pre-RFP intelligence system, or a performance guarantee. And they take 6–12 months to ramp.

**Response:** *"A good BD person is worth hiring — eventually. But consider what you're actually getting: a BD person at $150K all-in takes 6–12 months to ramp, and their signal flow is limited to their personal network. They don't come with a FERC filing monitoring system, an interconnection queue database, or a utility capex tracking layer. And they certainly don't come with a performance guarantee. We deliver the signal infrastructure and the pre-RFP positioning system on day one, and we're measured against a specific contract outcome. If you want to hire a BD person after the 180 days, you'll have a live pipeline and a proven signal map to hand them. That's a better situation than starting a BD person from scratch."*

---

### "What if you don't hit the guarantee?"

**Underlying concern:** Risk of paying $12K–$15K/month for six months and getting nothing.

**Reframe:** The guarantee has teeth — not as a marketing claim, but as a structural commitment.

**Response:** *"If we don't deliver two signed qualifying contracts within 180 days, we extend the engagement at no additional retainer cost until we hit the benchmark. We don't take performance fees on missed benchmarks — those are paid only on executed contracts. The retainer continues until we deliver. I want to be honest with you — we don't take engagements where we don't believe we can hit the guarantee. Part of the discovery process is confirming that the signal density in your target geography is sufficient and that your team has the bandwidth to participate in pre-RFP positioning. If those conditions aren't met, we'll tell you before engagement starts rather than take your retainer and fail. That's why the discovery process matters."*

---

### "180 days is too long to commit."

**Underlying concern:** They don't want to be locked into a six-month engagement with a vendor they don't know.

**Reframe:** 180 days is the minimum cycle for pre-RFP positioning to translate into signed contracts. It's not an arbitrary number.

**Response:** *"The 180 days is structural, not contractual preference. The pre-RFP cycle — from signal identification to positioning conversations to proposal submission to contract execution — is typically 90–150 days on its own. The guarantee requires two qualifying contracts, so the 180-day window accounts for two sequential or parallel cycles. If we compressed it to 90 days, the guarantee math doesn't work and we'd be setting an expectation we couldn't meet. What I can offer is a 30-day ramp period where we build the signal map and initiate first introductions before you make the full commitment. If you don't see credible pre-RFP signal activity in the first 30 days, you can exit without obligation. Does that address the concern?"*

---

### "We tried outbound before and it didn't work."

**Underlying concern:** They've been burned by a generic marketing or cold outreach vendor who generated noise but no contracts.

**Reframe:** Generic outbound fails because it doesn't have signal-based entry points. We never reach out without a specific project signal to anchor the conversation.

**Response:** *"I'd want to understand what 'outbound' meant in that context. If it was email campaigns, LinkedIn automation, or cold calling utility contacts without a specific project anchor — yes, that fails. It fails because there's no reason for a utility procurement contact to engage with a contractor they don't know based on a generic introduction. Every outreach we make is anchored to a specific project signal — a FERC filing, a capex announcement, an interconnection queue event. The conversation starts with 'we saw X happen on this project and wanted to understand your timeline' — not 'we'd love to be a resource for your future work.' The signal is what creates the entry point. Without it, outbound is noise. With it, it's a relevant technical conversation."*

---

### "This feels expensive."

**Underlying concern:** $12K–$15K/month feels like a large fixed cost, especially if they're uncertain about ROI.

**Reframe:** Frame against the contract economics. A single $500K contract on an EBITDA margin of 8–12% generates $40K–$60K in margin. Two contracts generate $80K–$120K in margin contribution. The retainer is recovered in the first contract.

**Response:** *"Let's do the math together. If we deliver two contracts at $500K each — the minimum guarantee — and your EBITDA margin is 8–10% on construction, that's $80K–$100K in margin contribution. The retainer at $15K per month over six months is $90K. Add a $25K performance fee per contract — $50K total — and the total program cost is $140K. Against $80K–$100K in margin on the minimum guarantee contracts, it looks close. But the guaranteed contracts are the floor, not the ceiling. The average contract value we're positioning for in Dominion and ERCOT territory is in the $1M–$3M range — not $500K. At $1.5M per contract and 10% margin, you're looking at $300K in margin contribution against a $140K program cost. That's the actual ROI model. If we can walk through those numbers with your actual margins, the math usually becomes clear."*
