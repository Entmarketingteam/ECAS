## GAP 1: Lead Magnet / Free Offer

**The strategic problem:** Cold outreach without a tangible proof-of-concept ask gets treated as "another BD vendor." ECAS needs a first step that proves the signal engine is real, requires a conversation to fulfill, and filters for ICP fit — simultaneously.

---

### Lead Magnet Option A: The Signal Snapshot
**Hook:** "We'll pull every live FERC interconnection queue entry, rate case filing, and PPA announcement relevant to your geographic footprint — and show you which ones represent active contract opportunities you're not pursuing. No charge. No pitch. Just the data."

**Format:** 1-hour working session delivered as a live screen-share or PDF report. ECAS runs the signal engine against the prospect's stated geography and specialty (substation, transmission, distribution). Output: 5-10 specific project signals with project name, owner, stage, estimated contract value, and decision-maker contact.

**Delivery mechanism:** Book a 45-minute "Signal Review" call. ECAS prepares the snapshot in advance. The call is the delivery. During delivery, the signals sell themselves — no pitch needed.

**Why it qualifies:** A contractor who books a Signal Review has implicitly acknowledged they don't have this intelligence. That's the ICP.

**Why it converts:** The Signal Snapshot is a preview of Week 1 of ECAS's full service. Buyers who see 7 live projects they weren't tracking will immediately understand what they're leaving on the table.

**Call-to-action language:** "Want to see every active grid project in your market right now? We'll pull the live data and walk you through it — takes 45 minutes, no prep required on your end."

---

### Lead Magnet Option B: The Missed Contract Audit
**Hook:** "In the last 12 months, how many enterprise grid contracts were awarded in your region that you didn't bid — because you didn't know they existed until the RFP dropped? We'll find them and show you what those signals looked like 90 days earlier."

**Format:** A retroactive signal audit. ECAS takes the prospect's geographic market and specialty, then traces 3-5 contracts that were awarded in the past 12 months and shows what the pre-RFP signal trail looked like: when the FERC filing appeared, when the interconnection queue entry went live, what the utility announced in their earnings call.

**Delivery mechanism:** 30-minute "Missed Contract Review" call. ECAS delivers a one-page summary. The call is the delivery.

**Why it qualifies:** The prospect has to tell ECAS their geography and specialty to get the audit. That's the intake form. Only contractors who lost contracts they should have won will feel the pain acutely — that's the buyer.

**Why it converts:** It's retrospective proof. You're not showing them what might happen — you're showing them what already happened. The gap between what they saw (the RFP) and what ECAS would have seen (the signal 90 days earlier) is visceral.

**Call-to-action language:** "Give us your market and specialty. We'll show you exactly which contracts from the last year you could have been positioned for — and what the signals looked like before anyone else saw them."

---

### Lead Magnet Option C: The Live Project Feed (7-Day Access)
**Hook:** "We'll give you 7 days of live access to the ECAS signal feed for your market. Every FERC filing, interconnection queue entry, utility rate case, and PPA announcement that hits your geography — delivered to your inbox daily. No commitment. See what you've been missing."

**Format:** ECAS configures the signal engine for the prospect's geography and specialty. For 7 days, they receive a daily digest of live signals. At the end of 7 days, ECAS schedules a debrief: "Here's what we found. Here's what it would look like if we turned this into contract motion."

**Delivery mechanism:** Email digest (daily, automated via n8n). Debrief call at Day 7.

**Why it qualifies:** A contractor who wants 7 days of live signals has already decided they want this intelligence. The question is just whether they hire ECAS or try to build it themselves.

**Why it converts:** After 7 days, the feed goes dark. The prospect has experienced the intelligence advantage and now has to decide: go back to flying blind, or engage ECAS. This is the SaaS trial model applied to a services business.

**Call-to-action language:** "Want to see the signal feed before you commit? We'll run it live for your market for 7 days — no charge, no obligation. If you don't see at least 3 projects you weren't tracking, we'll tell you so ourselves."

**Pillar 1: Signal Spotting (40% of content)**
What ECAS sees in real time from FERC filings, interconnection queues, rate cases, earnings calls. Not "here's how to read a FERC filing" — but "here's what this specific filing means for substation contractors in the Southeast." Teach the ICP to see signals they're missing. The operational mechanics of winning enterprise grid contracts — not generic sales advice, but contractor-specific BD: how pre-RFP positioning works, what a capture plan looks like for a $40M substation job, how to structure a relationship with a utility procurement team. This pillar establishes ECAS as a practitioner, not a marketer.

*Example topics:*
- "The difference between BD and contract capture — and why most EPCs are doing the first but not the second."
- "How pre-RFP positioning actually works on a $50M transmission project: the 90-day window, the right contacts, the right meetings."

Commentary on the power infrastructure market — what the IRA is doing to interconnection queues, where utility capital spend is concentrated, which regions are seeing the most grid modernization work. This positions ECAS as an intelligence firm with a point of view on the market, not just a vendor.

*Example topics:*

- "Which utility territories are seeing the most active substation work in 2026? The data tells a clear story."

**Pillar 4: Social Proof / Client Results (10% of content)**
Contract wins, project milestones, client metrics — framed in ECAS language. "A mid-tier EPC in the Midwest added 2 enterprise contracts in 5 months." No names unless clients consent. The goal is to make the 180-day guarantee feel like a documented pattern, not a marketing claim.
